## SECTION A — PLAYER ATTRIBUTES

### A1 — Physical Size (real units)

| Attribute | Unit | Range | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|-------|
| height | inches | 68–87 | 5'8" to 7'3". Display layer converts to feet/inches. |
| wingspan | inches | 67–92 | Independent from height. Can be shorter OR longer. |
| weight | lbs | 160–285 | Varies by position. Freshmen arrive underweight. |

**Wingspan relative to height — the most important physical trait:**
- wingspan > height + 3: exceptional length. Affects contests, steal range, passing lanes.
- wingspan ≈ height: average. No bonus or penalty.
- wingspan < height - 2: short arms. Penalty to contesting and post defense.

**Physical development in college:**
- Most players gain 5-20 lbs over career as they fill out
- 8% chance to grow 1 inch in height freshman-to-sophomore year
- 2% chance sophomore-to-junior year
- Never after junior year
- Strength increases naturally with weight gain

---

### A2 — Athleticism (1-1000 internal scale)

| Attribute | What it models | Key interactions |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| speed | Straight-line top speed | Transition, getting to spots, closing out |
| lateral_quickness | Change of direction | Perimeter defense, drive recovery |
| vertical | Leap height | Rebounding window, shot contests, rim finishing |
| strength | Physical force | Post battles, boxing out, fighting screens |
| explosiveness | First step burst | Drive initiation, beating closeouts, cutting |
| agility | Body control, balance | Finishing through contact, defensive recovery |

**Critical distinctions:**
- Speed ≠ Explosiveness. Long striders have top speed but slow first step. Twitchy guards have elite first step but average top speed.
- Lateral quickness ≠ Speed. Fast straight-line player can still get blown by on lateral cuts.
- Strength + Weight interact. A 180lb player with 800 strength still gets moved by a 250lb player with 600 strength.

**Athleticism development:**
- Peaks around sophomore-junior year for most players
- Speed and explosiveness decline slightly senior year for some arc types
- Strength increases with weight gain
- Vertical and agility more stable throughout career

---

### A3 — Skill Attributes (1-1000 internal scale, existing)

Offensive: catch_and_shoot, off_dribble, mid_range, three_point, free_throw, finishing, post_scoring
Playmaking: passing, ball_handling, court_vision, decision_making
Defensive: on_ball_defense, help_defense, rebounding, shot_blocking, steal_tendency, foul_tendency
Athletic (existing, now supplemented by A2): speed, lateral_quickness, strength, vertical, endurance

**Note on catch_and_shoot:**
catch_and_shoot is a SKILL that modifies make rate on three-point shot buckets.
It is NOT a shot type. Three-pointers are generated by shot bucket selection, not by this attribute.

---

### A4 — Mental Attributes (1-20 scale, existing + new)

**Existing:** basketball_iq, clutch, composure, coachability, work_ethic, leadership

**New additions:**

**ball_dominance (1-20, permanent)**
How much a player wants and demands the ball. Permanent personality. Doesn't develop significantly.
- 1-5: Role player. Never demands the ball. Happy to execute and get out of the way.
- 6-10: Moderate. Comfortable either way.
- 11-15: Wants the ball. Needs usage to stay engaged.
- 16-20: Must have the ball. Will force situations without it.

**usage_tendency (1-20, develops slightly)**
Separate from ball_dominance. Ball_dominance is personality. Usage_tendency is role acceptance.
A high ball_dominance player can develop lower usage_tendency with great coaching.

**off_ball_movement (1-20)**
Reads defense and gets to the right spot before the ball arrives.
Critical for low-usage efficient players. The dump-off finds him because he's already there.

---

### A5 — Portal and Carousel Attributes (1-20 scale, existing)
volatility, playing_time_hunger, home_loyalty, prestige_ambition, role_acceptance, coach_loyalty

**home_loyalty now connected to coordinates:**
With real coordinates on every player and program, home_loyalty becomes concrete.
A high home_loyalty recruit 60 miles from a program has real gravitational pull.
A high home_loyalty recruit 1,400 miles away faces real friction even with loyalty.

### A6 — Derived Physical Composites (calculated at matchup time, not stored)

**length_rating:**
`(wingspan - 72) * 15 + (height - 72) * 10`
Affects: shot contest radius, steal range, passing lane disruption

**frame_strength:**
`(weight / 220) * (strength / 500)`
Affects: post dominance, screen setting power, boxing out

**athleticism_composite:**
`(speed * 0.25) + (lateral_quickness * 0.25) + (vertical * 0.20) + (explosiveness * 0.30)`
Affects: defensive mobility, drive success, transition

### A7 — Positional Model

**Position is a coach label, not a physical constraint.**

Every player has:
- natural_position: generated at creation from physical profile + skill profile
- coach_position: assigned by current coach based on how they use the player

**Natural position generation:**
Driven by physical profile AND skill profile together. Not just size buckets.
A 6'6" player with elite ball handling and court vision generates as a point forward.
A 6'8" player with post skills and strength generates as a power forward.
The engine uses physical attributes directly for matchup resolution regardless of label.

**Same player, different coaches:**
A 6'8" wing gets labeled a "3" by a motion offense coach.
The same player gets labeled a "4" by a physical coach who posts him up.
Physical profile doesn't change. Role tendencies do.

---

### A8 — Player Fit Profiles

Every player gets a best_fit_styles list derived from actual attribute profile.
No player is so nondescript that no system values him.
Every D1 player is there for a reason.

**Archetype examples:**

*The tireless defender:* High lateral quickness, endurance, steal tendency, average offense.
Best fits: press defense, defensive identity coaches.
Useless in: pace and space, isolation offense.

*The catch-and-shoot specialist:* Elite catch_and_shoot and three_point, poor ball handling.
Best fits: motion offense, three-point heavy systems, off-ball roles.
Useless in: isolation systems, post-heavy systems that don't space the floor.

*The physical screener:* Elite strength, high weight, good off-ball positioning, poor scoring.
Best fits: motion offense (game-changing screens), pick-and-roll systems.
Statistically invisible. Analytically essential.

*The low-usage efficient big:* Low ball_dominance, low usage_tendency, high off_ball_movement,
high basketball_iq, high finishing. Shoots 60%+ on 4-5 attempts per game.
Only shoots dump-offs, put-backs, and wide open looks. Never forces anything.
Best fits: any system where the ball finds him. Disappears in heliocentric + low playmaking star.

*The high-IQ connector:* Elite court vision, elite passing, average everything else.
Best fits: motion offense, high-assist systems.
Makes everyone around him better. Stats don't show his impact.

*The depth pool specialist (tier 6-7):* Exactly one thing. Just one.
The 6'9" frame who changes shots by existing.
The catch-and-shoot only guard who shoots 42% from three.
The energy big who dives on every loose ball.
There is a system somewhere that values that one thing.

---

### A9 — Experience and Variance

**Physical attribute effectiveness by year in school:**
- Freshman: physical tools at 75% effectiveness in contact/positioning situations
- Sophomore: 85%
- Junior: 95%
- Senior: 100% + IQ interactions get bonus multiplier

**Youth creates variance, not just penalty:**
The freshman isn't just worse — he's less predictable.
Wide outcome distribution: high ceiling moments, low floor moments.
The alley oop nobody can stop. Then getting bodied out of post position the next play.

Senior: tight distribution around true skill level. Rarely spectacular. Rarely terrible.

**The Blair Principle:**
A senior with elite positioning, elite IQ, and grown man strength
beats the athletic freshman over a full game.
But the freshman has two or three moments that make the crowd go crazy.
Both outcomes are real. Both should happen.

## SECTION B — COACH ATTRIBUTES AND SLIDERS

### B1 — Existing Philosophy Sliders (1-100)
pace, shot_profile, personnel, pressure, philosophy, off_rebounding, shot_selection, rotation_size, rotation_flexibility

### B2 — New Philosophy Sliders (1-100)

**heliocentric_egalitarian**
1 = pure egalitarian. Ball moves. Everyone touches it. Five equal threats.
100 = pure heliocentric. Ball dominance through one player. Everything runs through him.

Key distinction: heliocentric = ball dominance, NOT shooting dominance.
A heliocentric system with a high-playmaking star still creates good shots for role players.
High ball_dominance + high passing + high court_vision = star who creates for others.
High ball_dominance + low passing + low court_vision = true ball stopper. Role players starve.

**set_plays_freelance**
1 = pure set play. Specific actions, executed repeatedly at high level.
System fit and role acceptance matter more than raw IQ.
100 = pure freelance. Players read defense and make decisions.
Basketball_iq and court_vision become primary offensive drivers.
Low IQ players are liabilities. High IQ players thrive.

**inside_out_outside_in**
1 = work inside first. Ball goes to post first. Guards facilitate.
100 = prioritize guards. Perimeter first. Bigs screen and protect rim.

### B3 — Existing Competence Ratings (1-20)
recruiting_ability, player_development, game_management, system_fit_eval,
staff_development, program_building, media_relations, player_relations

**game_management now connects to:**
- Burst system probability tilting
- Timeout effectiveness
- Substitution timing quality

### B4 — Substitution Style

**substitution_style attribute on coach dict:**

*strict_rotation:* 7-8 guys, predictable minutes, everyone knows their role.
Advantage: cohesion, conditioning, execution within defined roles.
Disadvantage: opponent can game plan for it. Cold player stays in anyway.

*hot_hand:* Rides whoever is playing well. Freshman hits three threes, plays 28 minutes.
Advantage: best performing players always on floor.
Disadvantage: bench never develops. Cold shooting nights leave team unprepared.

*shakeup_artist:* Goes deep when things go badly. Unexpected lineups. Zone out of nowhere.
Activates under pressure more than in wins.
Advantage: unpredictable, occasionally changes momentum completely.
Disadvantage: delays the inevitable, costs best players minutes in crunch time.

Combinations exist. Coaches can be strict_rotation in wins and shakeup_artist in losses.

### B5 — Situational Lineups

Every coach maintains mental models for:
- Closing lineup: best five for last 4 minutes of close game
- Press lineup: highest endurance + lateral quickness
- Foul trouble lineup: best available without foul-troubled players
- Comeback lineup: shakeup coaches go here when down big
- Blowout lineup: bench gets minutes, starters rest

## SECTION C — ENGINE MECHANICS

### C1 — Shot Buckets

Every field goal attempt is one of these. No blurring. No coin flips.

| Bucket | Description | Base make rate | Three? |
|--------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| at_rim | 0-4 feet. Layup, dunk, close finish | 58-65% | Never |
| floater | 4-10 feet. Mid-lane floater | 38-46% | Never |
| post | Inside 10 feet. Hook, turnaround, face-up | 44-52% | Never |
| mid_two | 10-18 feet. Mid-range jumper | 36-44% | Never |
| corner_three | Corner. 22-24 feet | 34-40% | Always |
| above_break_three | Above the break. 24+ feet | 32-38% | Always |
| putback | Offensive rebound put-back | 52-60% | Never |
| free_throw | Foul shots | per player FT attr | Never |

**Long two note:** Merged with mid_two initially. Split into separate bucket later if needed.

### C2 — Possession States

How offense enters each possession:
1. Set play / half court catch — default, no advantage created
2. Drive advantage created — guard beat his man
3. Post advantage created — big established position
4. Ball movement advantage — defense rotated, open shot created
5. Transition — fastbreak, numbers advantage

### C3 — Drive Creation Model

```
drive_success = (explosiveness * 0.40) + (speed * 0.20) + (ball_handling * 0.15)
              - (defense.lateral_quickness * 0.35) - (defense.explosiveness * 0.15)
              - (defense.on_ball_defense * 0.10)
```

If drive succeeds: shot bucket shifts toward at_rim or floater. Foul probability increases.
If rim protector present: decision point fires — attack, floater, or kick out.

**Kick-out decision:**
```
kick_out_chance = (court_vision * 0.5) + (passing * 0.3) + (basketball_iq/20 * 0.2)
```
If kick_out: open perimeter shot generated for best available shooter.

### C4 — Physical Matchup Integration

**At rim:**
defender.shot_blocking and vertical_advantage reduce make chance.
defender.length_rating reduces make chance proportionally.
Shooter's agility increases foul chance.

**Post:**
frame_strength battle determines advantage.
Strength advantage > threshold triggers double team check.
Double team + good passer = kick-out opportunity.
Double team + poor passer = forced shot or turnover.

**Perimeter:**
defender.length_rating creates contest penalty even at same height.
defender.athleticism_composite creates rush penalty (faster closeout = more rushed release).
catch_and_shoot bonus applies on catch situations.
off_dribble penalty applies on self-created shots.

**Floater:**
Specifically designed to get over length.
Elite shot blockers still contest via shot_blocking + vertical_advantage.
Shooter's agility bonus for body control.

### C5 — Turnover Model

**Turnover buckets:**
- bad_pass: poor decision, pressure, off-target. Sometimes stolen.
- ball_handling: lost dribble, fumble. Rarely stolen.
- charge: offensive foul. Never stolen.
- shot_clock: no shot found. Never stolen.
- intercepted: defense reads play. Always stolen.

**Steal attribution:**
On bad pass: steal_tendency + length_rating + court_vision (reads the pass)
On intercepted: steal_tendency weighted heavily
On ball handling: steal_tendency + lateral_quickness (strips the dribble)

### C6 — Rebound Model

```
reb_window = vertical + (height * 8) + (wingspan * 4)
```
Position bonus: C +150, PF +100, SF +50, SG 0, PG -50
Strength factor: frame_strength * 0.3 (holds position, seals opponents)

Defense has natural 1.2x multiplier — they're already in position.
Offensive rebounding bonus from coach off_rebounding slider.

### C7 — Foul Model

Fouls generated by context, not generically:

| Context | Foul type |
|---------|-----------|
| Drive to rim | Blocking foul — defender agility, positioning |
| Post battle | Reach-in/push — strength disparity |
| Perimeter contest | Hand-check — defender aggression, length |
| Rebound battle | Box-out foul — strength, position |
| Help rotation | Late foul — rotation speed, agility |

**Foul-out enforcement:** Players with 5 fouls removed from lineup.
Foul trouble (3+ fouls first half) reduces second half minutes.

### C8 — Assist Model

No play-by-play required. Possession attribution:

```
if shot_from_ball_movement: credit last passer
elif shot_from_drive_kickout: credit driver
elif shot_from_post_kickout: credit post player
elif shot_from_set_play_catch: probability check on creator
else: no assist (self-created)
```

Motion offense teams generate more assists naturally.
Isolation-heavy teams generate fewer.
AST/FGM ratio is an emergent output, not a tuned number.

### C9 — Game State Burst System

**Core rule: No artificial rubber band logic. Ever.**
Scores always reflect what's happening on the floor.
Burst system changes behavioral inputs that produce different outcomes through the engine.
It does not change make percentages.

**Trigger:** Scoring gap crosses ~15 points. Probabilistic, not automatic.
**Duration:** ~10-12 possessions (approximately 5 minutes). Then returns to baseline.
**Cooldown:** Cannot chain bursts. One per threshold crossing.

**Base probabilities (before modifiers):**
Team down big: 15-20% composure run, 15-20% collapse, 60-70% nothing happens.
Team up big: 10-15% lock in, 10-15% complacency, 70-80% nothing happens.

The rarity is what gives it weight.

**Modifiers that shift the weights:**
- Senior-heavy roster: composure run more likely, collapse less likely
- Freshman-heavy on road: collapse more likely, composure run less likely
- High game_management coach: tilts table in his favor
- Home vs road: home boosts composure run, road boosts collapse
- Talent gap: suppresses composure run probability for heavy underdog
- Tournament: slightly raises composure run for both teams

**During burst window — composure run:**
Turnover rate drops. Shot selection improves. Defensive intensity increases.
Better decisions produce better results organically.

**During burst window — collapse:**
Turnover rate spikes. Shot quality drops. Defensive breakdowns increase.
Bad decisions produce bad results organically.

**Timeout mechanic:**
Timeout called at right moment increases burst probability for caller.
Maybe 15% becomes 35% for next stretch. Not guaranteed. Increased.
Timeouts are finite. Using them early costs you when you need them late.
High game_management coach gets better boost, better timing.
Poorly timed timeout: small chance fires wrong way — opponent gets momentum.

## SECTION D — SYSTEM INTERACTIONS

### D1 — Coaching Style Penalties and Tradeoffs

Every coaching style has an optimal personnel profile.
When roster matches style: full upside.
When roster mismatches style: penalty that can exceed the benefit.

**Press/trap:**
Upside: turnovers, transition buckets, rhythm disruption.
Penalty beaten: defense out of position, transition buckets surrendered.
Penalty mismatch: guards gas out, turnovers spike late game.
Beaten by: elite court vision, good passing, composed ball handlers.
Requirement: high lateral quickness, endurance, basketball IQ guards.

**High pace:**
Upside: more possessions, wears opponents down.
Penalty: defense never set, easy transition buckets surrendered.
Penalty mismatch: low endurance roster collapses in second half and late season.
Beaten by: elite half-court defensive teams.

**Aggressive off-rebounding:**
Upside: second chance points.
Penalty: exposed in transition every missed shot.
Beaten by: fast-breaking opponents.

**Post-heavy offense:**
Upside: efficient high-percentage looks, draws fouls.
Penalty: bleeds shot clock, collapses if post player in foul trouble.
Beaten by: physical defenses, teams that double early.
Worst mismatch in the game: post system without a real post player.

**Zone defense:**
Upside: hides poor athletes, disrupts timing offenses.
Penalty: vulnerable to patient ball movement and three-point shooting.
Beaten by: motion offense with shooters.

**Full court pressure:**
Upside: forces turnovers, controls tempo.
Penalty: exhausts own players, especially short rotations.
Deep roster can press all game. Short rotation is gassed by fourth quarter.

**Isolation offense:**
Upside: best player gets his best shot.
Penalty: rest of roster goes cold, predictable late game.
Beaten by: physical defense that doubles, teams that take away one option.

**Mismatch amplification:**
Penalties compound. A press team with poor athletes doesn't just get fewer steals.
Turnovers spike, transition buckets surrender, fatigue builds, opponent's offense gets easier.
A severe mismatch can make a good coach look like a bad one.

### D2 — Ball Dominance System Interactions

High ball_dominance in egalitarian system:
Forces shots, undermines ball movement, slightly higher late-clock turnovers.
Portal trigger if mismatch persists.

Low ball_dominance in heliocentric + high playmaking star:
Still gets his shots. Star finds him on dump-offs.

Low ball_dominance in heliocentric + low playmaking star:
Nearly disappears. Star never looks for him.

Five high ball_dominance players on same roster:
Chemistry disaster. Recruiting failure. Takes two classes to fix.

### D3 — Roster Fit Health

Every program has roster_fit_health calculated each season.
High fit health: penalties reduced, bonuses amplified.
Low fit health: penalties amplified, bonuses reduced.
Most important number connecting recruiting to engine outcomes.

After a coaching change: fit drops immediately.
Takes 2-3 recruiting classes to fully rebuild.
This is why coaching changes hurt programs beyond just stability.

Portal wave after coaching change: not just about loyalty.
Players whose attributes don't fit the new system have a real reason to leave.

### D4 — Neutral Site Atmosphere

Hard ceiling: 5% maximum modifier. Felt, not manufactured.

**Crowd split calculation:**
Distance from each fanbase to venue (driving distance matters most).
Prestige differential (high prestige programs travel nationally).
Regional basketball culture (some conferences travel more aggressively).

**Modifier tiers:**
- Own arena: 4-5%
- 75%+ neutral crowd: 2-3%
- 60-74% lean: 0.5-1%
- 45-59%: true neutral, 0%
- Reverse for road team perspective

**What atmosphere affects:**
Free throw percentage (road team reduced by roughly half the modifier).
Burst system probability (tilts toward crowd-supported team).
Turnover rate (slight increase for road team under pressure).

**What atmosphere does NOT affect:**
Field goal make rates directly.
Talent differential.
Atmosphere matters most in close games.

## SECTION E — GEOGRAPHIC SYSTEM

### E1 — Coordinate Foundation

Every program: latitude/longitude from real city location.
Every recruit: assigned to real high school with coordinates.
Every neutral site: latitude/longitude.
Distance calculated in real miles via haversine formula.

### E2 — Recruit Location Generation

**State population distribution:**
Each state has talent distribution across metro areas based on real population
and basketball participation rates. Oklahoma example:
OKC metro ~32%, Tulsa metro ~28%, Lawton ~6%, remaining cities split the rest.

**High school database:**
~2,000-3,000 real high schools nationally that produce D1 talent.
Tier 1-2 recruits come disproportionately from major basketball programs.
Tier 3+ come from broader pool including smaller programs.
The diamond in the rough from a small town is generated occasionally.

### E3 — Distance Effects

Recruiting: home_loyalty * distance friction = regional gravity.
Neutral sites: distance from each fanbase determines crowd split.
Assistant coaches: regional effectiveness bonus within their territory.

## DESIGN PRINCIPLES (never violate these)

1. Physical attributes amplify skill. They never replace it.
2. Skill and IQ let players route around physical limitations entirely.
3. Experience creates consistency. Youth creates variance.
4. Every coaching style has a cost. No free upside.
5. Every player fits somewhere. No player is truly nondescript.
6. Scores always reflect what's happening on the floor. No rubber band logic.
7. Atmosphere matters. 5% ceiling. Never more.
8. Formulas are starting points. Calibration targets are gospel.
9. Heliocentric = ball dominance. Not shooting dominance.
10. The Blair Principle: craft and positioning beat raw tools over a full game.
    But the freshman has his moments. Both are real.
